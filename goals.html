Goals and Objectives

Short-Term Goals

  • Complete the CCNA certification course before the end of the summer
  • Build 2–3 cybersecurity lab projects (e.g., phishing detection, log analysis, network defense)
  • Update and polish my GitHub with relevant code samples and documentation
  • Get more involved in online cybersecurity communities and forums like LetsDefend and TryHackMe
  • Start journaling or documenting my study and progress habits for better discipline

Long-Term Goals

  • Obtain multiple industry-recognized certifications such as CompTIA CASP+, CISSP, or Azure Security Engineer Associate
  • Land a security-focused role in a government agency, defense contractor, or tech company
  • Develop my own cybersecurity training content to help beginners break into the field
  • Create a mentorship group or blog for first-generation college students in tech
  • Own a business focused on security consulting, training, or managed services

How NSU and Other Organizations Are Helping

NSU’s cybersecurity program gives me a well-rounded foundation in both technical and business-related skills. The hands-on projects and access to virtual labs let me simulate real-world cyber scenarios. Being surrounded by peers and faculty who genuinely care about student success has made a big difference.

Outside of school, I’ve benefited a lot from organizations like Blacks in Technology (BIT), which has connected me with professionals already working in the industry. I also draw motivation from YouTube creators and mentors who share their IT journeys. These resources remind me that consistency beats perfection and that the path to success in tech is a marathon, not a sprint.

With the combined support of my school, my community, and my own drive, I feel equipped to chase bigger goals while helping others rise with me along the way.
