Interview Q&A

What development tools have you used?

I’ve used Visual Studio Code, Wireshark, Cisco Packet Tracer, Burp Suite, and Windows Server Admin tools. I also worked with proprietary tools like TOAST and FIDO at H&R Block.

What languages have you programmed in?

HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Java, and Python. I’ve also worked with command-line scripting in PowerShell and Bash for automation tasks.

What are your technical certifications?

CompTIA A+, Security+, and I’m currently studying for the CySA+ and CCNA certifications.

What do you do to maintain your technical certifications?

I earn CEUs through webinars, hands-on labs, and practice exams on platforms like CompTIA’s CertMaster, LinkedIn Learning, and LetsDefend.

How is your education helping you prepare for the job market?

I’m majoring in Computer Information Systems with a concentration in Cybersecurity. My coursework covers real-world tools, security frameworks, and IT fundamentals. It’s also teaching me to communicate technical information clearly.

How would you rate your key competencies for this job?

I’d rate my technical troubleshooting, adaptability, and communication skills as strong. I’m constantly learning and improving through both hands-on work and formal study.

What are your IT strengths and weaknesses?

Strengths: Problem-solving, communication, and network troubleshooting. Weakness: I tend to over-research before acting, but I’m learning to trust my knowledge and make confident decisions faster.

Tell me about the most recent project you worked on. What were your responsibilities?

I helped upgrade computers from Windows 10 to Windows 11 across multiple locations using internal imaging tools. I troubleshot any installation errors, ensured all endpoints were functional, and documented each upgrade in the asset system.

What challenges do you think you might expect in this job if you were hired?

Learning new internal systems quickly and staying up to date with evolving cybersecurity threats. But I’m confident in my ability to adapt and take initiative to stay ahead.

How important is it to work directly with business users?

Very important. Building trust and understanding user needs leads to better IT solutions and faster resolutions. I’ve learned that patience and clear communication can make all the difference.

Why do you want to work in cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity combines my love for problem-solving with my desire to protect people and systems. I want to be on the frontlines of defending networks and contributing to a safer digital world.

How do you stay current with technology?

I follow cybersecurity news, watch YouTube tutorials, take online courses, and network with professionals through groups like Blacks in Technology.

Can you explain a time you handled a difficult technical issue?

At H&R Block, a system was constantly crashing during imaging. I discovered a driver conflict and resolved it by manually adjusting the deployment script, saving hours of repeated troubleshooting for future use.
